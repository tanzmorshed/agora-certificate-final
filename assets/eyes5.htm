    Details

    Lay down the line with our best-selling, longwearing, matte black LIQUID EYE-LIE-NER in Punk. The superfine, flexible felt tip guides your line without tugging or flaking for subtle-to-bold precision that brings any look to life.
     

    LONGWEAR THAT DOESN'T LIE
    This 24-hour longwearing liquid eyeliner doesn't show any signs of leaving with a smudge, slip, and flake-proof formula.

     

    BEND THE TRUTH
    The flexible felt tip guides your line and bends the truth with an effortless, no slip application to take your look from subtle to bold.

     

    KEEP YOUR DARK SECRET
    Matter-than-matte black pigment leaves the deepest finish for bold payoff every time.

     

    24-hour longwear

    Ingredients

    Aqua/Water/Eau, Methylpropanediol, Styrene/Acrylates Copolymer, Styrene/Acrylates/Ammonium Methacrylate Copolymer, Cellulose, PEG-60 Hydrogenated Castor Oil, Coco-Glucoside, Poloxamer 407, Citric Acid, Phenylpropanol, Phenoxyethanol, Potassium Sorbate, Sodium Benzoate, Caprylyl Glycol, Sodium Laureth-12 Sulfate, Sodium Lauryl Sulfate, BHT, Sodium Dehydroacetate, Black 2 (Cl 77266/[Nano])

    Fill Weight: 1.0 ml / 0.03 fl oz

    How-To

    STORE liner upside-down for optimal ink flow.

    STAMP the liner into your upper lash line.

    CREATE the base of your wing by drawing a line from your outer corner toward your hairline.

    CONNECT the tip of the wing to your upper lash line and fill in to complete the look.

    Artistry

    Start by creating a natural winged eyeliner look as your base. For an edgy, graphic look that Lady Gaga loves to rock, trace over the wing shape with a powder eyeshadow or Glam Attack. This adds layered dimension and seals in the shape. To complete this look, outline the wing shape with LIQUID EYE-LIE-NER for a bold frame.

     

    For A Louder Look:

    EYE-LIE-NER is your artistry tool and your face is the canvas. This eyeliner was designed with versatility in mind, so embrace experimenting and creating your own unique looks. Use the tip and gentle pressure for precise artistry and angle the liner on its side with heavier pressure for bolder, more intense looks. The rules are entirely up to you.
